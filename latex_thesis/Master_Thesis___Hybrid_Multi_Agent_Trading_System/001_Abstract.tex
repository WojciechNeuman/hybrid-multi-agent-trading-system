This work develops and evaluates a hybrid multi-agent system for algorithmic trading, in which each agent relies on a distinct artificial-intelligence technique. It is trained and evaluated on hourly Bitcoin (BTC/USDT) data under a strict, leakage-controlled walk-forward protocol, using OHLCV prices, technical indicators, microstructure and market-structure features, and external sentiment and cross-asset signals.

\vspace{3mm}

Four learned models from different paradigms were built: a gradient-boosting model (LightGBM), a temporal convolutional network (TCN), a patch-based Transformer (PatchTST), and a selective state-space model (Mamba), together with several rule-based agents. The final fund is a predeclared roster of five, curated for diversity and validation-window behaviour: LightGBM, the TCN, Mamba, a trend follower, and a cross-asset dominance-rotation rule, combined by a leak-free capped inverse-volatility allocator. PatchTST was built but excluded, because its out-of-sample directional accuracy is no better than chance even after walk-forward retraining. Other approaches that did not make the fund, including deep reinforcement learning and evolutionary rule generation, are reported as honest negatives.

\vspace{3mm}

Over a two-year out-of-sample window spanning three market regimes, the fund returned $+53.7\%$ at a Sharpe ratio of $1.50$ with a maximum drawdown of $-9.2\%$, ahead of Bitcoin buy-and-hold (Sharpe $0.09$) and the S\&P~500 (Sharpe $1.16$). The result is robust but modest. A block bootstrap places the Sharpe firmly above zero, yet the capped allocator barely edges an equal-weight blend (Sharpe $1.48$), so the value lies in holding a diverse roster rather than in the allocation rule. A learned adaptive coordinator failed to beat the simple risk-parity baseline, and the highest-returning single agent did not survive a random-bracket test for genuine skill. The results suggest that combining heterogeneous AI methods can deliver more stable, regime-balanced performance than any single approach, with the benefit coming from disciplined diversification rather than a sophisticated controller. A practical continuation of this work would be live deployment, with its real-world execution costs.
